SAFETAG

The Security Auditing Framework and Evaluation Template for Advocacy Groups

SAFETAG is an open source organizational risk assessment and audit framework

"Pen-testing" is not the way forward

What works in the corporate world doesn't scale to the NGO world. Aggressive and exhausting hacking of small NGOs does not support effective long-term changes

So what is SAFETAG?

SAFETAG is an organizational security auditing and assessment tool designed for the NGO community. It currently exists as an auditor guide with 12 thematic areas and over 50 specific exercises tailored for small organizations with limited technical resources, with a strong focus on agency-building and organizational change dynamics throughout the process. It has a corresponding curricula to train new auditors, and is available in English and Spanish. Internews has used and adapted SAFETAG over its 3+ year life in dozens of audits ourselves, and it has been adopted in various forms by the ISC Project, Frontline Defenders, NDI, and many independent auditors as well.

Understanding Capacity and Building Agency

SAFETAG differs from other organizational risk assessment practices by understanding and focusing on each organization's ability to change and their current capacity to adopt more secure practices.

What's next?

We are at an important point in SAFETAG's life; where we are working with this broader community of organizational security practitioners to rebuild SAFETAG in a much more modular structure, with the core "minimal viable audit" as the unifying theme and approach -- with partners building customized "playlists" and creating new modules to be suit the communities they work with.
